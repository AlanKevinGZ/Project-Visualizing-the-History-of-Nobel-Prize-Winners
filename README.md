#  Visualizing the History of Nobel Prize Winners

An exploratory data analysis project using Python to uncover trends and patterns in Nobel Prize data from 1901 to 2023.

---

##  Project Overview

The Nobel Prize is one of the most prestigious international awards in the world. Since 1901, it has been awarded annually in six categories: **Chemistry, Literature, Physics, Physiology or Medicine, Economics, and Peace**.

This project explores the Nobel Prize dataset provided by the [Nobel Prize API](https://nobelprize.org/) to answer key questions about gender representation, birth countries, decade trends, and repeat winners.

##  Key Questions Explored

1. **What is the most commonly awarded gender and birth country?**
   - Most awarded gender: **Male**
   - Most common birth country: **United States of America**

2. **Which decade had the highest ratio of US-born Nobel Prize winners?**
   - Answer: **The 2000s**

3. **Which decade and category had the highest proportion of female laureates?**
   - Answer: **2020s – Literature**

4. **Who was the first woman to receive a Nobel Prize, and in what category?**
   - **Marie Curie** – Physics

5. **Which individuals or organizations have won more than one Nobel Prize?**
   - Marie Curie, née Sklodowska
   - International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC)
   - Linus Carl Pauling
   - UNHCR
   - John Bardeen
   - Frederick Sanger
